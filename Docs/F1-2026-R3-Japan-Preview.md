# F1 2026 — R3 Japan Preview & Pick Strategy

*Aramco Japanese GP — Suzuka Circuit*
*Based on post-R2 championship standings and corrected manufacturer data*

---

## Track Character

High-speed, high-downforce, unforgiving. Sectors 1 and 3 reward aero balance; the long back straight rewards power. Historically punishes unreliable cars hard. No sprint — clean 1× scoring, multipliers are the only lever.

---

## Manufacturer Angle

The corrected manufacturer picture changes Japan significantly:

- **Honda home race → Aston Martin**, not Red Bull. If Honda brings performance upgrades for Suzuka (they historically do), Alonso and Stroll are the beneficiaries. Both sit at 0pts = 2× multiplier and are currently unpicked by anyone in the league.
- **Red Bull Ford** gets no home-race boost at Suzuka. Their reliability problems on a demanding, high-speed track are a real concern.
- **Mercedes AMG HPPT** customers are 4 teams deep — Mercedes, McLaren, Williams, Alpine. The power unit looked dominant in R1–R2. Suzuka's long straights could extend that advantage further.

## Drivers to Watch

**Oscar Piastri (P12, 2×)** — McLaren has clearly had raw pace but two DNSs buried him in the standings. Suzuka should suit the car, and at 2× with a clean run he's the highest-ceiling pick in the field. The question is whether the team has fixed whatever caused the withdrawals.

**Aston Martin — Alonso (P16) / Stroll (P17), both 2×** — the Honda home-race narrative is real. Aston have looked awful on pace, but if Honda brings an upgrade package for Suzuka and the car isn't fundamentally broken, this is where it shows. Enormous multiplier, almost zero competition for this pick slot from other players.

**Pierre Gasly (P7)** — Alpine on Mercedes power has been the quiet story of the season. Suzuka's long back straight plays to Mercedes power. Gasly has scored in both rounds and is becoming a reliable mid-pick.

**Max Verstappen (P9, 2×)** — everyone will want the bounce-back story at a track he's dominated historically. But Red Bull Ford has shown reliability issues precisely at moments of high engine demand. High consensus pick = high risk if he DNFs again.

## Key Risk Scenarios

- **Verstappen DNF again** — anyone avoiding him gains on the consensus; anyone holding him drops
- **Piastri/Norris DNS again** — McLaren reliability remains the biggest unknown entering the round
- **Aston Martin / Honda upgrade lands** — if they score points at their home-race engine circuit, whoever took the Hail Mary wins the round
- **No sprint** — no half-point safety net; every pick is all-or-nothing on race day
