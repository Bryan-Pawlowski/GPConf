# F1 2026 — Early Season Trends (Rounds 1–2)

*Based on GPConf data: Australia GP + China Sprint + China GP*

---

## Mercedes Dominance

**The story of 2026 so far is Mercedes.** Russell and Antonelli have swept both race wins and both pole positions, swapping the top two positions between rounds:

- Australia: Russell P1, Antonelli P2 (qualifying and race)
- China: Antonelli P1, Russell P2 (qualifying and race)

Combined 98 championship points between them — a 31-point gap over Ferrari already. In practice they weren't the obvious pacesetter (FP1 Australia was Ferrari/Red Bull 1-2-3), but they consistently found more in qualifying trim and executed cleanly on race day.

---

## Ferrari — Fast but a Step Behind

Leclerc topped FP1 in Australia, Hamilton was top-4 in every session across the weekend, yet they can't get near Mercedes come Sunday. Both Ferrari drivers are remarkably consistent (P3/P4 in both races, P2/P3 in China sprint) but there's a ~15 second gap to Mercedes by the end of races. Combined 67 points.

---

## McLaren — Qualifying Pace, Race Fragility

Norris and Piastri routinely qualify P5/P6 but are converting almost nothing:

- Piastri: DNS in China race, DNS in Australia race
- Norris: 10pts from Australia, DNS in China race, 5pts from sprint
- The McLaren is genuinely quick — Piastri led FP2 in Australia — but race-day DNSs are costing them heavily

---

## Oliver Bearman — Midfield Standout

**17 points** from P7 (Australia) and P5 (China) makes Bearman the best of the rest by a wide margin. He's beating established midfielders and outscoring his teammate Ocon 17–0. Haas appears to have a very solid baseline car this year.

---

## Red Bull / Verstappen — Reliability Crisis

Verstappen was P3 in FP1 in Australia, suggesting good single-lap pace, but:

- Australia: P6 (salvaged points but well off the front)
- China: DNF after 11 laps

Hadjar at Red Bull also DNF'd in Australia. Combined just 8 points for Red Bull — Verstappen is already P9, tied with Lawson (Racing Bulls). The Red Bull Ford power unit or chassis reliability looks like the core problem.

---

## Aston Martin & Cadillac — Off the Pace

Both new-manufacturer entrants are struggling badly:

- **Aston Martin** (Alonso + Stroll): 0 points, multiple DNFs in both races, slowest practice lap times
- **Cadillac** (Perez + Bottas): 0 points, DNFs in Australia, lapped repeatedly in China

Aston Martin is 2+ seconds off the pace in practice — these aren't just reliability issues, it's a fundamental car deficit.

### Round 1 — Australia (No Prior Standings)

R1 had no pre-race standings so all multipliers were 1×. Scores were tight (5–15 pts). Spoonahh led with 15pts by spreading picks across five drivers who actually scored (Norris, Bortoleto, Russell, Verstappen, Lindblad). WildGingerBear scored 12pts with a bold Lindblad P1 pick that paid off.

### Round 2 — China (Sprint Weekend)

China introduced two scored events per pick — the **sprint at 0.5×** and the **feature race at 1.0×**, with a **2× max standings multiplier**. A driver scoring in *both* events effectively delivers 1.5× the base pick score. This is where the field split apart.

**WildGingerBear — 27pts (round high):** Picked Lawson (P21 in standings = 2× multiplier) as P2 pick. Lawson scored in *both* sprint and race, combining for 12pts from that single pick alone. Also landed Colapinto (P1 pick, 7.5pts) and Hadjar. Three high-multiplier scoring picks.

**Spoonahh — 24.5pts:** Bearman as P1 pick (scored in both sessions = 7.5pts), Lawson as P3 pick (scored in both = 9pts), Hadjar as P4 pick (4pts). Methodical targeting of consistent scorers with high multipliers.

**Ding Chavez — 0pts:** Every pick (Verstappen, Ocon, Bortoleto, Stroll, Bottas) returned zero — a combination of DNFs, DNSs, and finishing outside the points. On a sprint weekend with two scoring chances, a clean wipeout is especially costly.

### Key Patterns

- **The sprint multiplier is decisive.** Picking a driver who scores in *both* sprint and race yields 1.5× the calculated pick score — equivalent to a full additional 0.5 pick score on top of the race result.
- **High-multiplier midfielders** (Lawson P21, Hadjar P22 pre-China) were the highest-value picks. Players who targeted them earned 9–12pts from a single slot.
- **Verstappen was the trap.** Three players put him in their top-2 for China — all got 0 (DNF after lap 11).
- **Bearman (P7 standing going into China)** rewarded both players who picked him with 6–7.5pts, scoring in both sessions.
- **Spoonahh leads by half a sprint point** (39.5 vs 39.0) — one pick scoring in the sprint vs not.
